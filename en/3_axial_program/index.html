Points of a three-point Gaussian quadrature are just the roots of the third degree Legendre polynomial. Points of a five-point Gaussian quadrature are the roots of the fifth degree Legendre polynomial.

The accuracy (eps) of integral calculation is achieved by dividing the interval [gauss_a; gauss_b]. A current interval [aa; bb], its length вЂ“ В«yВ». Integral is calculated as a sum of integrals over intervals wich are parts of the interval [gauss_a; gauss_b]. To obtain the definite integral over current interval the five-point Gaussian quadrature (s5) and the three-point Gaussian quadrature (s3) are used. If absolute value of the difference between s5 and s3 less than absolute value s5 multiplied by eps then s5 is added to the sum and the next interval is in work. Else the current interval is divided by two. If the length of new interval is less than the interval [gauss_a; gauss_b] length multiplied by 10-12, then the integral can not be calculated with required accuracy. But in our case the integrands have high-order derivatives and small absolute values and the program of calculation works reliable enough. Accuracy in radians given by default is excess and ensures accuracy of coordinates shown on the monitor at least.
